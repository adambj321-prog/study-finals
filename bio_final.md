## Scientific Method

- **Observation** = using your 5 senses to gather info
- **Inference** = a logical interpretation based on observations and knowledge
- **Qualitative data** = categorical → bar graph
- **Quantitative data** = numerical → line graph

### Steps of the Scientific Method
1. Observation
2. Finding a problem
3. Forming a hypothesis
4. Conducting a controlled experiment
5. Collecting and analyzing data
6. Drawing conclusions
7. Communicating results

### Key Definitions
- **Fact** = a confirmed observation proven to be true
- **Hypothesis** = an educated prediction that can be tested
- **Theory** = a well-supported scientific explanation backed by evidence

### Graphing
- X axis = independent variable
- Y axis = dependent variable
- Line graph = quantitative/numerical data
- Bar graph = qualitative/categorical data

## Cell Cycle and Mitosis

- Multicellular organisms undergo mitosis to: make more cells, wound repair, replace old/dead cells, reproduction, fight infection
- Interphase = growth, copying of DNA, more organelles made
- Daughter cells in mitosis are identical to parent cell
- **Cytokinesis difference:** Animal cells = membrane pinches in two; Plant cells = cell wall splits the cell

## Meiosis

- **Sexual reproduction** = 2 parents (XX and XY), offspring get 50% traits from each
- **Asexual reproduction** = 1 parent, offspring 100% identical to parent
- **Diploid (2n)** = 2 of each chromosome (humans = 46)
- **Haploid (n)** = 1 of each chromosome (humans = 23)
- **Sister chromatids** = 2 identical chromatids that form together into a chromosome
- **Homologous chromosomes** = 2 pairs of chromosomes with same size and shape

### Stages of Meiosis
- **Interphase** = chromosomes replicated from sister chromatids
- **Prophase** = nuclear envelope disappears, spindle fibers form, chromosomes become visible
- **Metaphase** = homologous pairs line up in center; random arrangement → genetic variation
- **Anaphase** = spindle fibers shorten, homologous chromosomes separated
- **Telophase + Cytokinesis** = nuclear membrane reforms, chromosomes decondense, spindle fibers disappear; each new cell has 2 sister chromatids not identical due to crossing over

### Sources of Genetic Diversity
- **Crossing over** = homologous chromosomes exchange DNA during meiosis
- **Independent assortment** = homologous chromosomes pair randomly → many combinations
- **Random fertilization** = any sperm can fertilize any egg

### Somatic cells vs. Gametes
- **Somatic cells** = body cells (skin, nerve, bone, blood) — diploid
- **Gametes** = sex cells (egg/sperm) — haploid

## Genetics and Genetic Diseases

### Key Terms
- **Gene** = segment of DNA that determines an individual's characteristics
- **Alleles** = different forms of a gene
- **Dominant** = expressed even when paired with a recessive allele
- **Recessive** = expressed only when both alleles are recessive
- **Homozygous** = 2 identical alleles
- **Heterozygous** = 2 different alleles
- **Genotype** = the allele combination (e.g. RR, Rr, rr)
- **Phenotype** = the physical expression (e.g. tall or short)
- **Complete dominance** = one allele completely dominant (RR)
- **Incomplete dominance** = neither allele dominant, mixture results (e.g. red + white = pink)
- **Codominance** = both alleles expressed simultaneously
- **Polygenic** = traits controlled by more than one gene, showing a range of phenotypes

### Inheritance Patterns
- **Autosomal dominant** = only one copy needed to cause disorder; affects males and females equally
- **Autosomal recessive** = two copies needed; not sex-linked
- **Sex-linked** = gene located on sex chromosome

## DNA Replication, Transcription, and Translation

### DNA Replication
- **Helicase** = unzips the DNA
- **DNA Polymerase** = copies DNA into a new strand
- **Ligase** = connects the Okazaki fragments
- **Semiconservative** = each new DNA molecule has one old strand and one new strand

### DNA vs. RNA Structure
| Feature | DNA | RNA |
|---|---|---|
| Sugar | Deoxyribose | Ribose |
| Bases | G, C, A, T | G, C, A, U |
| Strands | Double | Single |

### Types of RNA
- **mRNA** = carries instructions from DNA to ribosome
- **tRNA** = brings amino acids to the ribosome
- **rRNA** = makes up the ribosome

### Transcription & Translation
- **Codon** = group of 3 bases on mRNA indicating which amino acid to add

### Point Mutations
- **Substitution** = one base switches for another, changes the codon
- **Nonsense mutation** = creates a premature stop codon
- **Insertion** = base added, causes frameshift
- **Deletion** = base removed, causes frameshift
- **Frameshift** = bases become unreadable, changes amino acid sequence

## Evolution

- **Darwin's natural selection** = species change over time through natural selection

### Evidence for Evolution
- **Fossil record** = preserved remains in ice, amber, tar pits, sedimentary rock
- **Homologous structures** = same structures on different species → common ancestor (divergent evolution)
- **Analogous structures** = different structures, same function → convergent evolution
- **Vestigial structures** = structures used by ancestors but no longer functional
- **Comparative embryology** = similarities in early developmental stages → common ancestor
- **DNA/protein sequences** = similar sequences → closer relation

### Types of Natural Selection
- **Directional** = one extreme trait is most fit
- **Stabilizing** = average/middle trait is most fit
- **Disruptive** = both extremes are most fit

### Speciation / Reproductive Isolation
- **Behavioral isolation** = different behaviors prevent reproduction
- **Geographic isolation** = physical barrier separates populations
- **Temporal isolation** = different mating times prevent reproduction

- **Artificial selection** = humans selectively breed for desired traits

## Digestive System

### 4 Stages of Food Processing
1. **Ingestion** = taking food into the mouth
2. **Digestion** = converting food into small molecules cells can absorb
3. **Absorption** = nutrients move into circulatory system
4. **Elimination** = disposing of waste

- **Mechanical digestion** = physical (chewing, squeezing)
- **Chemical digestion** = molecules breaking down food (starch → glucose)

### Digestive Enzymes
- **Pepsin** = breaks down protein (stomach)
- **Amylase** = breaks down carbs (saliva + small intestine, made in pancreas)
- **Lipase** = breaks down fats (small intestine, made in pancreas)
- **Trypsin** = breaks down protein (small intestine, made in pancreas)

### Pathway of Food
Mouth → Pharynx → Esophagus → Stomach → Small Intestine → Large Intestine → Rectum → Anus

### Key Organs
- **Liver** = makes bile (accessory organ)
- **Pancreas** = makes lipase, trypsin, amylase; contains buffers to neutralize stomach acid
- **Epiglottis** = prevents food from going down the trachea
- **Esophagus** = peristalsis moves food to stomach
- **Small intestine** = final digestion + absorption; villi increase surface area
- **Large intestine** = reabsorbs water

### Digestive Diseases
- **Celiac disease** = gluten causes immune system to attack villi
- **Diarrhea** = not enough reabsorption in large intestine
- **Constipation** = too much reabsorption in large intestine
- **Ulcer** = thin spot in stomach lining damaged by acid
- **Acid reflux / heartburn** = stomach acid backs up into esophagus

## Circulatory System

### Components of Blood
- **Red blood cells** = carry oxygen
- **White blood cells** = fight pathogens
- **Platelets** = help blood clot

### Blood Vessels
- **Arteries** = carry blood away from the heart (thick elastic walls, high pressure)
- **Veins** = carry blood to the heart (thinner walls, lower pressure)
- **Capillaries** = smallest vessels; single-file blood cells; exchange of gases, nutrients, waste

### Pathway of Blood
Superior vena cava → Right atrium → Right ventricle → Pulmonary artery → Lungs → Left atrium → Left ventricle → Aorta

- Blood is **oxygenated in the lungs** and **deoxygenated in the body**

### Circulatory Diseases
- **Hypertension** = chronic high blood pressure
- **Atherosclerosis** = buildup of fats/cholesterol on artery walls restricting blood flow
- **Heart attack** = blood flow to the heart severely reduced
- **Ischemic stroke** = blood clot blocks vessel carrying blood to the brain
