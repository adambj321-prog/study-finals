## Short-Answer Questions

1. What specific action did Hiel the Bethelite take that provoked the fulfillment of a centuries-old curse?
2. How did the marriage between Achab and Jezebel change the religious landscape of the Northern Kingdom?
3. Identify the three specific idolatrous acts Achab established in Shomron.
4. What was the nature of the decree Eliyahu delivered to Achab during their first encounter?
5. How was Eliyahu physically sustained during his time at Nachal Kerit?
6. Why did Hashem eventually command Eliyahu to leave Nachal Kerit and go to Zarephath?
7. What was the widow of Zarephath doing when Eliyahu first encountered her?
8. How did the widow describe her dire situation before the miracle of the flour?
9. Describe the specific instructions Eliyahu gave the widow regarding preparing the bread.
10. What was the duration of the miracle of the jar of flour and the jug of oil?
11. Why did the widow feel that Eliyahu's presence caused her son's death?
12. How does Rashi explain the concept of a person's merits being "weighed" when a high-level tzadik is present?
13. What physical actions did Eliyahu perform to revive the widow's son?
14. According to Talmud Sanhedrin, what are the "three keys" not typically given to messengers?
15. Explain the argument: "Two keys in the hand of the student and one in the hand of the Master."
16. What is the symbolic connection between "dew" and the "resurrection of the dead"?
17. How did the revival of the boy effectively force Eliyahu to end the drought?
18. What was the widow's final realization after her son was returned to her alive?
19. In the "Doctor's Bag" analogy, what does the bag represent in the context of Eliyahu and Hashem?

## Essay Questions

1. **The Tension of Prophecy** — Analyze the conflict between Eliyahu's zeal for justice (the drought) and Hashem's attribute of mercy. How does the revival of the boy serve as a bridge between these two?
2. **The Role of Hospitality** — Compare the widow's willingness to provide for Eliyahu despite her poverty with the spiritual state of Israel's leadership. What does her character reveal about the requirements for a miracle?
3. **Providence and Geography** — Discuss the significance of Hashem sending Eliyahu specifically to Zarephath in Zidon. Why send the prophet to the heart of the culture he was opposing?
4. **The Transformation of the Prophet** — Track Eliyahu's development from his first decree to Achab to his prayer for the widow's son. How does his interaction with human suffering change his prophetic stance?
5. **Covenants and Curses** — Explore the theme of "The Word of Hashem" as it applies to both Yehoshua's curse on Jericho and Eliyahu's decree. How do these events demonstrate the permanence and power of the prophetic word?

## Glossary of Key Terms

| Hebrew Term | English | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| Baal (בַּעַל) | Baal | Canaanite/Phoenician deity of storms and fertility championed by Jezebel |
| Asherah (אֵׁשֵׁרָה) | Asherah pole | Cultic object used in worship of the Canaanite goddess |
| Tishbi (תִּשְׁבִּי) | Tishbite | Town of origin for Eliyahu, or designation of his status as a resident |
| Nachal Kerit (נַחַל כְּרִית) | Brook of Kerit | Location where Eliyahu was sustained by ravens and water |
| Orevim (עֹרְבִים) | Ravens | Birds commanded by Hashem to bring bread and meat to the prophet |
| Tzarfat (צָרְפַת) | Zarephath | Phoenician city near Zidon where Eliyahu stayed with the widow |
| Kad (כַּד) | Jar / Barrel | Container for flour that did not empty during the drought |
| Tzapachat (צַּפַּחַת) | Jug / Cruse | Container for oil that remained full by miracle |
| Aliyah (עֲלִיָּה) | Upper Chamber | Room where Eliyahu lived and performed the revival of the boy |
| Techiat HaMeitim (תְּחִיַּת הַמֵּתִים) | Resurrection of the Dead | Miraculous returning of life to a deceased person |
| Tal (טַל) | Dew | Moisture Eliyahu initially withheld; associated with future resurrection |
