## Biology 9A

**Multiple Choice / Short Answer**

1. A scientist observes that plants near a window grow taller than those kept in the dark. She predicts that more light causes faster growth. Is this statement an *observation* or a *hypothesis*? Explain the difference.

2. During meiosis, homologous chromosomes exchange segments of DNA. What is this process called, and how does it contribute to genetic diversity?

3. A human somatic cell has 46 chromosomes. How many chromosomes would you find in a gamete produced by that person? What term describes this chromosome number?

4. A man with blood type AB mates with a woman who is homozygous recessive (type O). Use a Punnett square to determine the possible blood types of their offspring. What type of inheritance does this demonstrate?

5. A substitution mutation in the 2nd base of a codon creates a premature stop codon. What specific type of mutation is this, and what effect does it have on protein synthesis?

6. What enzyme is responsible for *unzipping* the DNA double helix during replication? What enzyme connects the Okazaki fragments?

7. Arrange the following in the correct order of food processing: **Elimination, Absorption, Digestion, Ingestion**. Then identify which enzyme breaks down fats, and where it is produced.

8. Compare the pathway of *oxygenated* vs. *deoxygenated* blood through the heart. Which side of the heart handles each?

9. Explain the difference between **homologous structures** and **analogous structures**. What type of evolution does each suggest?

10. A population of moths lives in a forest with medium-colored bark. Over time, both very light and very dark moths survive better than medium-colored moths. Which type of natural selection is this?

## Macbeth — Accelerated Literature & Composition 9A1

**Quote Identification & Analysis**

1. "Fair is foul, and foul is fair." — Who says this, and in what act/scene? How does this quote set up one of the play's central themes?

2. "Out, damned spot, out, I say!" — What does this scene reveal about Lady Macbeth's psychological state? How does it connect to the theme of guilt?

3. "Life's but a walking shadow, a poor player / That struts and frets his hour upon the stage / And then is heard no more." — What philosophical meaning does Macbeth express here? At what point in the play does he say this, and why is the timing significant?

**Short Answer**

4. What is Macbeth's *fatal flaw*, and how does it qualify him as a tragic hero?

5. How does Lady Macbeth use Macbeth's masculinity against him? Give one specific example from the play.

6. Explain the theme of **Natural Order**. What specific event disrupts it, and what consequences follow?

7. Compare how Macbeth and Lady Macbeth each respond to guilt over the course of the play. Who appears more affected, and how does each character's guilt manifest physically or psychologically?

**Essay**

8. Shakespeare suggests that ambition without moral limits leads to destruction rather than fulfillment. Using evidence from the play, analyze how Macbeth's ambition leads step by step to his downfall.

## Mechina Tanach — The Prophet Eliyahu and the Reign of Achab

**Short Answer**

1. What are the three central sins of Achab, and why is each spiritually significant?

2. Describe how Eliyahu was physically sustained while hiding at Nachal Kerit. Why did Hashem eventually command him to leave?

3. What did the widow of Zarephath say when Eliyahu first asked her for bread? What does her response reveal about her situation?

4. According to the Talmud, what are the "three keys" not normally handed to a messenger? How does this concept apply to Eliyahu's situation at Zarephath?

5. Why did the widow believe that Eliyahu's presence in her home caused her son's death? How does Rashi explain the weighing of merits in the presence of a tzadik?

6. What specific physical actions did Eliyahu perform to revive the widow's son? Cite the relevant pasuk (Melachim I 17:21).

**Essay**

7. *The "Doctor's Bag" Analogy* — Explain the analogy and what it reveals about the relationship between Eliyahu's decree (the drought) and Hashem's attribute of mercy. How does the revival of the son resolve the conflict between these two forces?

8. Hashem sends Eliyahu specifically to Zarephath in Zidon — the very cultural heartland of Jezebel. Discuss the theological and symbolic significance of this choice of location.
