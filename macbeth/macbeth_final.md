## Major Themes

### Fair is Foul, Foul is Fair
Evil disguises itself behind a false mask. Goodness and morality are difficult to recognize. Nothing and nobody in the play is as it seems.

### Fate and Free Will
Does Macbeth act of his own free will, or is he controlled by the witches, Lady Macbeth, or his own impulses?

### Masculinity and Gender Identity
Lady Macbeth wants to "unsex" herself and reject "womanly" traits like kindness and sympathy. She and Macbeth both use challenges to manhood as manipulation.

### Evil
The play explores the nature of evil through Macbeth, Lady Macbeth, and the witches — cruelty, violence, and betrayal throughout.

### Ambition
Macbeth's desire to be king leads him to murder Duncan. Ambition leads to destruction rather than happiness.

### Guilt
Both Macbeth and Lady Macbeth are consumed by guilt:
- Macbeth sees a floating dagger and the ghost of Banquo
- Lady Macbeth sleepwalks and imagines blood on her hands
- Both suffer from insomnia

### Natural Order
There is a correct natural order to the world. When God's appointed king is murdered and a usurper takes his place, chaos follows.

### Tragic Hero
Macbeth is the classic tragic hero — consider what his fatal flaw is and how it leads to his downfall.
